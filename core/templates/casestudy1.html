Process Optimization

WBI Investments

SUMMARY

When an SMA manager converts their investment platform to an ETF strategy, backoffice systems need to be implemented and processes need to enhanced.

When WBI, an independent investment advisor with a 30-plus year history converted their separately managed account platform into an exchange traded ETF strategy, they know that they needed to scale their technology in order to support more advisors. This radical transformation to a fee-only advisor platform meant that WBI needed to scale their technology and backoffice support. Instead of hiring more people to support higher volumes, WBI wanted to do more with the tools they had. After interviewing team leaders across the business, we discovered that many people had been doing their jobs "that way for 20 years".

Legacy systems - i.e. Access databases and excel spreadsheets - were created as an adhoc request, but quickly became a process to support a new business function...30 years later, it was unclear to most users, how data travelled throughout the organization.

After defining the goals - to create a single data source of to support all functions of the organization, it became clear that we needed to document the processes and data flow into and out of the firm, develop a rules-based workflow manager, and train people on how to use these new systems.

At the end of the project, WBI was able to support over 12,000 accounts and launch 6 new ETFs.
